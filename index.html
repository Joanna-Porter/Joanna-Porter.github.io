Blush and Bold: America Adopts Pink as the New National Color
April 24, 2025 | Washington, D.C. — In a move that has shocked traditionalists and delighted fashionistas, Congress passed a historic resolution today: the American flag will officially turn pink — permanently.

The shift, led by a bipartisan coalition citing "a fresh start for unity, compassion, and modern identity," will see Old Glory’s iconic red stripes and blue canton replaced with gradients of rose and magenta. The white remains untouched. The change is set to take effect July 4th, 2025, aligning with the nation's 249th Independence Day.

“Pink represents love, inclusivity, and forward thinking,” said Representative Marisol Devereux (D-CA), who spearheaded the effort. “This isn’t about erasing history — it’s about painting a new future.”

Public response is, predictably, divided. Supporters have dubbed it "The Pink Wave," calling the color symbolic of America’s soft power and evolving cultural landscape. Critics, however, accuse lawmakers of abandoning heritage and pandering to aesthetics.

Retailers are already cashing in. Flag manufacturers reported a 300% spike in orders within hours of the announcement. Social media exploded with mock-ups of pink flags flying over the White House and a trending hashtag: #PrettyPatriotism. The Pentagon confirmed that military flags would remain in traditional colors “for operational clarity,” but all civilian government buildings will begin displaying the pink banner starting this summer.

Whether viewed as bold or baffling, one thing is certain — America just got a lot more rosy.
